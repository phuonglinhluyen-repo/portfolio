Introduction

Hi,
I'm Linh.

I'm a first-year student at Università Bocconi, Milan, studying Economics and Management for Arts, Culture and Communication. Before Bocconi, I grew up in Hanoi, graduated from the Amsterdam High School for the Gifted, and spent three years building communities, running PR campaigns, and writing stories for live audiences.

I started my first fan page because I noticed a gap — no one had built a Vietnamese-language community for one of the best League of Legends players in the world. So I built it. That instinct — to see what's missing and fill it — is how I approach everything.

What connects all of it: I care deeply about the people on the other side of the screen, and I know how to write for them.

What I Learned

Data tells you what works. Instinct tells you why.

A post about a loss outperformed a highlight reel because it was honest. I learned to use data to confirm instinct, not replace it.

Tone is the hardest thing to get right.

My highest-performing posts were written as someone who genuinely cared — not as someone managing a page. That distinction is everything.

Events turn followers into community members.

Giveaways and polls were moments people started introducing themselves to each other in the comments. That shift is what I was building toward.

What I Learned

Brand consistency across a team is harder than solo work.

Managing 10 people all writing content meant constantly re-aligning on voice and tone. I learned to create briefs and feedback loops rather than doing everything myself.

Outreach is community management before the community exists.

Getting partners involved early meant the audience was already primed when registration opened. Building relationships before you need them is the actual skill.
